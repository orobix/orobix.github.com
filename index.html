Who we are and what we do.

Shirop is our platform for data collection, management and real-time analysis.

It enables us to provide customized solutions for a wide range of data intensive problems, from scalable clinical record systems to big data analytics, machine learning applications or large image analysis and simulation systems.

 

The products

Shirop has been used to create several solutions now in production use at our clients.
In parallel, we have developed two products based on the platform, namely Shirop Nephrology and Shirop Pediatrics

The technology

At its core, Shirop leverages on a number of state-of-the-art open-source technologies, including relational, document-based or graph databases, real-time computation systems, machine learning and multidimensional data visualization.
Shirop is designed from the ground up to fit a wide range of scales, from embedded solutions to large-scale applications deployed on the cloud.

The Shirop infrastructure is mainly developed in Clojure, a modern Lisp implementation running on the Java Virtual Machine. We are strong supporters of functional programming, separation of identity and state, composition over inheritance and libraries over frameworks.
With this approach, we can address specific needs and follow their evolution over time.

While Shirop is used as an internal platform at Orobix, we are releasing some of its building blocks as open-source.
Our primary aim is to spread the technology, rather than keeping it in our little corner.

Hirop

Hirop is an open source library written in Clojure. It is a data layer sitting between the client application and the database backend aimed at taking the complexity out of writing distributed applications where data consistency is a strong requirement.

It provides a git-like workflow for dealing with local snapshots ("contexts") of data. It streamlines the management of conflicts arising from concurrent writes and provides graph semantics to the data in a context, taking the complexity away from the development of the application code.

Hirop is designed to maximize reusability of data definitions by separating relationships from the actual data. It also allows the shape of data to evolve over time without needs for migrations and it provides hooks for data validation and transformation.

It works with multiple database backends, from relational databases like PostgreSQL (both with full schema and JSON columns) to document-based databases like CouchDb, to graph-based databases like OrientDb. In all cases, all operations on contexts are ACID compliant and backends remain querable natively, so that data can be accessed from third party applications.

For more insight, see: hirop on github.

the Vascular Modeling Toolkit

vmtk is a library for image analysis, image segmentation, geometric analysis, mesh generation, pre- and post-processing or numerical simulations.
Its development started back in 2004 and it is now a fairly well known tool in the bioengineering community, being used by several research groups around the world. Thanks to its liberal license, some of its algorithms (one for all, centerline computation) have been incorporated in third party software.

As a company, we provide consulting and training on projects leveraging on vmtk or on projects in related areas (e.g. VTK, ITK, 3DSlicer, OsiriX). We partner with research institutions and industrial subjects to create new algorithms, customized workflows or full-fledged applications for a clinical audience.

We are currently working at extending the reach of vmtk. We like its focus on pipelining composable scripts to create complex workflows. We'd now like to make users' life easier.
